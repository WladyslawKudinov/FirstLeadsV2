# Competitive landscape for FirstLeads: an unoccupied niche

**No company in Russia or globally positions "product hypothesis validation through real sales outreach" as a standalone, primary service.** This is the single most important finding of this research. The intersection of lean validation methodology (hypothesis → experiment → learn) and B2B cold outreach execution (email, LinkedIn, Telegram, calls) represents a genuine white space. Adjacent players exist in two non-overlapping worlds — validation agencies that rely on interviews and surveys, and leadgen agencies that optimize for pipeline volume — but nobody bridges them. The Russian outreach market is small (~100–120 million RUB/year) but grew **2–3× in 2025**, signaling rising demand for the underlying channel.

## TASK 1: Direct competitors — the category is nearly empty

Extensive search across both Russian and English queries confirmed that **no company frames its core value proposition as "validation through sales."** What exists are adjacent players that partially overlap from two directions: outreach agencies that incorporate hypothesis testing into their methodology, and GTM agencies that include outbound as one component of broader services.

### Strogo Bureau (strogoburo.ru) — closest Russian competitor

Cold outreach agency specializing in email and Telegram B2B outreach that explicitly tests multiple hypotheses (ICPs, messaging angles, value propositions) through campaigns and measures results. In one documented case, they **tested 12 hypotheses and generated 19 leads** for an IT company. They target narrow-market B2B companies in Russia — IT, SaaS, digital agencies, consulting. Pricing is not publicly listed. They also offer courses on cold outreach alongside agency services. Key distinction from FirstLeads: they position as a lead generation agency, not a hypothesis validation service. No structured "validation report" or product-discovery framework is visible. They lack an explicit methodology linking outreach results back to product decisions. Market presence is modest — boutique agency with active blog and content marketing. Published a comprehensive market analysis of the Russian cold outreach landscape.

### Polza Agency (polzaagency.ru/emailoutreach) — Russian email outreach with hypothesis framework

Claims to be "#1 в РФ по email-outreach." Uses **HADI+ICE methodology** to test hypotheses through cold email campaigns with personalized letters and A/B testing. Delivered **700+ qualified leads to 100+ companies in 2024**. Pricing starts at **79,000 RUB/month** (~$800–900). Promises 15–50 leads/month with launch in 5 days. Targets established B2B businesses, SaaS/IT startups, financial and consulting services. Strengths include transparent pricing, 85+ projects, 11 years of founder experience, and membership in the Marketers Guild. The HADI hypothesis testing is about messaging optimization, not product-market fit discovery — a critical distinction from what FirstLeads does.

### WB-Tech (wbtech.ru) — IT dev shop with published validation methodology

Published a detailed methodology for "product hypothesis validation and finding first B2B clients" using LinkedIn Sales Navigator, QuickMail, data enrichment tools, and Zapier-based automation. **Most conceptually aligned** with FirstLeads' value proposition — their content demonstrates deep understanding of the approach. However, this is not their primary business: WB-Tech is a custom development shop, and validation appears to be a side consulting offering. No dedicated landing page, no pricing, and unclear how many clients have used this specific service. Blog post dates from 2023; unclear if actively offered.

### Predictable Revenue "Outbound Validation" (predictablerevenue.com) — closest global analog

Founded by Aaron Ross (author of the sales classic *Predictable Revenue*), this B2B outbound agency created a specific sub-program called **"Outbound Validation."** It maps client hypotheses about new customer segments, breaks them into testable hypotheses, then deploys email campaigns to test — running **up to 20 experiments/month**, combining 3–7 market segments × 3–7 messaging angles. Positive respondents get routed immediately to client sales; neutral/negative respondents receive follow-up qualification questions. They claim to find the right message + market **"up to 5× faster."** Key limitation: this is a sub-offering within a larger outbound service, not a standalone product. Primarily serves companies with existing products expanding to new markets, not pre-product validation. Canadian company, likely priced at **$5K–$15K/month** based on industry norms.

### SalesRoads (salesroads.com) — phone-based market validation

U.S.-based B2B appointment setting company (17+ years) with an explicit **"Market Research & Validation"** use case. Their SDRs reach ideal prospects via phone, test messaging, and uncover real purchase drivers to "confirm demand before scaling sales." Pricing: **$5,500–$9,500 per 4-week engagement**. Phone outreach provides the highest-fidelity demand signal, but validation is a secondary use case — their core business is appointment setting/SDR outsourcing. 100% U.S.-based team with 14+ years average SDR experience.

### SalesCaptain (salescaptain.io) — international GTM agency

B2B outbound & GTM agency that builds go-to-market motions from scratch: ICP definition, buyer journey mapping, messaging, and execution of multichannel campaigns. Works with YC/Techstars-backed startups through Series C. Pricing starts at **$5,000+ per project** with monthly fees around **$3,500**. **60+ organizations served, $10M+ in pipeline generated.** Clutch #1 B2B Outbound Agency. Closest international competitor to FirstLeads' model, but focused on pipeline generation rather than structured product learning.

## TASK 2: Indirect competitors across five categories

### 2a. Custdev agencies collect opinions, not buying signals

**custdev.online does not exist** as an active website — multiple search queries returned no results. The closest alternatives are custdev.agency (Mercury Research) and procustdev.ru.

The Russian custdev agency market is fragmented with no dominant player. **None of the identified agencies provide buying signals (willingness-to-pay validation through actual purchase attempts).** All operate in the interview → analysis → recommendations paradigm. The closest to buying signal methodology is **IT-Agency**, which conducts "solution interviews" where they show solutions and ask about purchase readiness — but this remains stated intent, not validated behavior.

**Price landscape for custdev services in Russia:**

- **AskUsers** (askusers.ru): From **33,000 RUB** for basic CustDev with 10 respondents, 5-day turnaround. Originally a UX testing platform; CustDev is secondary. Primarily collects opinions via panel-based respondents.

- **Geerafe** (geerafe.ru/fastcusdev): Express packages — **155,000 RUB** (1 week, competitor analysis + survey + VPC), **225,000 RUB** (1.5 weeks, adds trends and behavioral insights), **295,000 RUB** (2 weeks, full package with CJM and positioning). Addresses market viability questions but through surveys and desk research, not purchase validation.

- **Pro-Custdev** (procustdev.ru): Claims #1 in audience research, **500+ large projects**. Team of 5–7 per project, ~70-page deliverable, 2-week turnaround. Pricing not public. Studies "reasons for purchase and refusal" but retrospectively through interviews.

- **IT-Agency** (it-agency.ru/services/custdev): From **550,000 RUB/month**, 4–8 week timeline. Major digital agency (20 years, 189 clients). The only agency that explicitly mentions checking if people are "ready to buy" during solution interviews — closest to willingness-to-pay, though still interview-based stated intent.

- **Custdev.agency** (Mercury Research): Since 2020, 10 researchers, 20+ clients. Delivers lead profiles, content maps, product line ideas. Heavy focus on info-business/online courses rather than B2B tech.

- **Белый Квадрат** (kvadrat-digital.ru): St. Petersburg agency since 2016. Emphasizes that CustDev is "not about interviews, but about concrete applicable solutions." Recommends 20+ total interviews minimum. Opinion-focused methodology.

**Key finding:** The gap between custdev agencies and FirstLeads is structural. Custdev agencies answer "what do people say they want?" FirstLeads would answer "will people actually engage when offered this product?" — a fundamentally different and stronger signal.

### 2b. Product strategy and GTM consultants are fragmented and advisory

The Russian product consulting market is dominated by individual ex-product-managers from top tech companies offering freelance consulting. No consolidated player combines strategy with actual market testing.

**ProductConsulting.ru** is the most relevant — focused on IT companies, offering product strategy, OKR implementation, **B2B user acquisition**, and sales funnel building. Founded by someone with Tinkoff and Sber background. Explicitly mentions "building online sales channels" — goes beyond reports into process building. Pricing not public.

**GoExpert.pro** is a marketplace connecting **400+ top experts** from Yandex, Ozon, Tinkoff, and Avito with companies needing on-demand product/strategy/tech advice. Partnered with Skolkovo Foundation. Quality varies by individual expert. Not a consulting firm with unified methodology.

**Neuromap.tech** focuses on digital and product strategy for large enterprises. **500+ people trained** with claimed $100M+ impact. Premium strategic sessions, 30+ proprietary frameworks. More "teaching to fish" than "fishing for you."

**Product Market Lab / Сергей Колосков** runs consulting and courses with a Telegram channel "Fresh Product Manager" (**20K subscribers**). Acts as "secret CPO" for companies. Primarily education/mentoring focused rather than hands-on market entry.

**Key finding:** Nobody in Russian product consulting goes from strategy to actual market testing through outreach. The gap between "here's your product strategy document" and "here's proof that the market wants this" remains unbridged.

### 2c. Leadgen agencies lack product thinking

**No leadgen agency in Russia explicitly positions around "hypothesis validation" or "product-market fit testing."** All sell volume (meetings, leads per month).

**LeadConnect** (leadconnect.ru) is the closest — offers a **6–8 week sprint pilot testing 12–20 hypotheses** through outbound, with explicit SaaS/IT expertise (ICP, JTBD, BANT/MEDDICC qualification). Claims "+120 SQL in 3 months, CPL down 34%" for a SaaS client. Multi-channel: SEO, PPC, outbound, ABM. Fixed fee + KPI pricing model. But they still frame everything as "lead generation," not product validation.

**Лидология / Leadology** (leadology.ru) has a dedicated startup track ("we'll help get first paying customers"), explicit SaaS segment focus, and 9 years on market with 100+ clients. But more marketing strategy than outbound — no cold email/outreach as primary offering.

**SaaS Founders / IT Founders** (saasfounders.net): LinkedIn lead generation specifically for IT companies, team of **30+ lead generators**, claiming 2,000+ leads/year at average check $50K+. Ukrainian origin (Kyiv-based). Positioned as volume LinkedIn leadgen for IT services selling to Western companies — no product validation positioning.

**Coldy.ai** — Russian cold email platform with "Done for You" managed service. Includes Russian company database (Kontour.Kompas integration), compliant with 152-ФЗ. Both a SaaS tool and managed service, but positioned as operational infrastructure, not strategic partner.

**ABSLead** (abslead.ru): Turnkey cold email campaigns, claims "ROMI 1300%," results in 5 weeks. Very operational, not strategic.

**Respondo** (respondo.ru): Email platform/service with published case of **39 qualified leads for fintech** with 8.25% reply rate. Tool-first, not advisory.

### 2d. Venture studios validate through pilots, not outreach

Russian venture studios are dominated by **corporate players** (MegaFon, MTS, Kaspersky, Rosselhozbank) creating studios for their own strategic needs. Independent studios are few. Almost universally equity-based (**30–80% equity**).

**Admitad Projects** (admitad.pro) — part of Mitgo Group ($300M+ revenue). Closest to outreach-based validation with a centralized **"Sales and Marketing House"** and a 50+ step tracksheet methodology tracking from "contact with audience → first sale → repeat sales → scaling." Invests up to **$1.5M per project** (up to 3M RUB for idea validation). Takes up to **80% equity**. 30+ investments over 7 years, 150+ ideas in backlog, 13+ active projects. E-commerce focus limits relevance for general SaaS.

**ADV LaunchPad** (lpd.vc) — studio of ADV Group (top-5 Russian advertising group). Initial investment **1.5–3M RUB** for projects with confirmed customer problem. Partners with Fort Ross Ventures for follow-on funding. 700+ applications received, ~15 projects/year funded. AdTech/MarTech specific.

**GenerationS** (generation-startup.ru) — Russia's largest corporate accelerator platform. **600+ projects per program pipeline.** Does not invest or take equity. Acts as matchmaking between startups and corporate clients (Северсталь, РЖД, МКБ, X5 Retail). Helps startups access corporate pilots but doesn't do outreach itself.

**Key finding:** Studios validate through product development, customer interviews, and pilot projects with corporate partners — **not through systematic outbound sales outreach**. A clear gap exists for a service bridging leadgen agencies (who do outreach but lack product thinking) and venture studios (who have product thinking but don't execute outreach).

### 2e. Global analogs confirm the white space

The validation market globally splits into two non-overlapping worlds:

**Validation world** (Prelaunch.com, Horizon/GetHorizon.net, Bundl, The PMF Company, Netguru PVS, Forum Ventures) uses landing pages, surveys, interviews, and fake doors. None do cold outreach.

- **Prelaunch.com** creates landing pages where visitors place **$5–10 deposits** to prove purchase intent. Strong for consumer hardware. Relies on paid ads, not outreach. B2C/hardware focused.
- **Horizon** (gethorizon.net) runs multivariate landing page tests ("pretotyping") with social media/search ad traffic. Enterprise clients include Bosch and Virgin Pure. SaaS + managed service. B2C/FMCG oriented.
- **Bundl** (bundl.com) does corporate validation sprints (1–2 weeks) for Forbes 2000 companies (HSBC, Nike, Chanel, Microsoft). Experiments are landing pages/surveys/interviews. Likely **$50K–$200K+ per engagement**.
- **The PMF Company** (thepmfcompany.com) offers PMF sprints (~3 weeks) for early-stage founders through interviews, surveys, and retention audits. Small boutique operation.

**Outreach world** (CIENCE, Belkins, SalesHive, Martal Group) focuses on booking meetings and pipeline. None position around validation.

- **CIENCE** (cience.com): GTM system setup at **$5,000 flat** + ~$2,000/month + $499/month platform. SDRs at $1,500–$5,500/month each. Declining market position.
- **Belkins** (belkins.io): Major appointment-setting agency via email outreach. Strong reputation. Pure pipeline focus.
- **SalesHive** (saleshive.com): **117,000+ meetings booked**, 1,500+ companies served, **$2.1B+ in pipeline**. Proprietary AI platform. Pure pipeline focus.
- **Growth Rhino** (growthrhino.com): Helps early-stage B2B startups get traction through cold outreach — ICP definition, lead lists, messaging. Startup-focused but still positioned as leadgen.

**The gap is confirmed globally:** Nobody combines lean validation methodology with B2B cold outreach to real decision-makers as a primary, standalone service.

## TASK 3: Manual workarounds and where they fail

### Founder-led outreach — the universal default

**Prevalence:** Considered the foundational approach for every early-stage B2B startup. Industry consensus is near-universal: "Every successful B2B startup is built on founder-led sales." Founders typically need to send **50 outreach messages, make 10 calls, do 5 demos per week** — consuming 50–70% of their time for months. **Cost:** Minimal direct cost ($60–100/month for tools) but enormous opportunity cost — a CEO's time at $200–500/hour equivalent represents **$40K–100K/month** in opportunity cost. **Where it breaks down:** Technical founders struggle with sales mindset ("approach it like engineering"), it doesn't scale beyond a handful of conversations per week, iteration is slow (months to get enough data), and founders tend to hear what they want to hear due to prospect politeness. Sustained cold outreach is psychologically draining for non-sales people.

### Warm referrals — biased but high-trust

**Prevalence:** Very widespread. Almost half of B2B tech vendors acknowledge customer referrals as their leading marketing tactic. Referred customers show **37% higher retention**. **Cost:** Near-zero monetary cost. Moderate time investment in relationship nurturing. **Where it breaks down:** Creates echo-chamber validation — existing clients' contacts share similar characteristics. Referred contacts are more polite and positive, inflating interest signals. Relationship capital is finite; each referral request "costs" social capital. Most critically, **this approach fails for validating new ICPs** — if exploring a new market segment, existing clients' networks won't reach the right people. Warm intros trickle in slowly and cannot be accelerated.

### Fake door test (landing page + ads) — weak signal for B2B

**Prevalence:** Widely used in B2C, growing but problematic in B2B. Companies like Buffer and Semrush have used it. **Cost:** Landing page $0–500 (Tilda, Unbounce); LinkedIn Ads average **$3.94–$10+ CPC** (SaaS average $8.04/click); cost per lead **$150–400** on LinkedIn; minimum meaningful budget **$3,000/month**. Total realistic test: **$2,000–5,000** for 2–3 weeks. **Where it breaks down for B2B:** Clicks ≠ buying intent when B2B purchases involve **6.8 stakeholders and 211-day average customer journeys**. No qualitative feedback — you learn nothing about *why* people did or didn't engage. Brand reputation risk ("tricking customers"). Cannot simulate multi-stakeholder approval processes, budget allocation, or competitive evaluation. Most teams optimize for volume when they should optimize for qualification.

### Freelance SDR hire — knowledge gap is fatal

**Prevalence:** Moderately widespread and growing. The outsourced SDR market reports 25–30% cost reduction vs. in-house. **Cost in Russia:** **80,000–200,000 RUB/month** (~$800–2,000). **Globally:** $3,000–8,000/month retainer, or $150–600 per qualified meeting (mainstream B2B), $900+ for enterprise. 2–4 week ramp time eats into short engagements. **Where it breaks down:** A freelance SDR doesn't understand the product deeply enough for nuanced conversations about a yet-to-be-built product — they're trained to sell proven products, not validate hypotheses. Incentive misalignment: SDRs optimize for meetings booked, not insight quality. Critical nuances in customer feedback get lost in translation through the middleman. **SDR annual turnover runs ~40%**, making short engagements even riskier.

**Russian-specific tools:** Coldy.ai (Russian B2B cold email, Kontour.Kompas integration, 152-ФЗ compliant), Respondo.ru (cold email with Russian market optimization), Salesnotes.ru (training and consulting). Global tools have "problems with Russian-language support, high cost, and need additional adaptation for the Russian market."

Ongoing: **5–15 hours/week**. The tools are built for pipeline generation, not structured hypothesis testing — no built-in framework for tracking which hypotheses are validated or when to pivot.

### All workarounds share the same structural weakness

None are designed for hypothesis validation. All require significant founder time. Feedback quality is inconsistent with no standardized methodology. The "last mile" problem persists — getting a conversation is hard, but extracting actionable product insights requires a different skillset than selling. The Russian market adds friction: global tools don't work well for Russian B2B, and the local ecosystem remains underdeveloped.

## TASK 4: Strategic questions answered with evidence

### Does "validation through sales" exist as a formalized service category?

**No. The category does not exist as a formalized, standalone service — in Russia or globally.** This is confirmed across dozens of searches in both languages. The closest global example is Predictable Revenue's "Outbound Validation" sub-program, which runs up to 20 email experiments per month testing market segments and messaging. But even this is positioned as a feature within their larger outbound sales service, not as a standalone product. SalesRoads' "Market Research & Validation" is similarly a secondary use case within appointment setting. In Russia, no company was found that explicitly offers this as a service. **This represents a genuinely unoccupied niche.**

### Which Russian leadgen agencies say "we help validate hypotheses / enter new markets"?

**None position this way explicitly.** LeadConnect (leadconnect.ru) comes closest with their 6–8 week sprint pilot testing 12–20 hypotheses, but they frame it as leadgen optimization. Лидология (leadology.ru) has a startup track promising "first paying customers" but executes through digital marketing, not cold outreach. Strogo Bureau (strogoburo.ru) tests hypotheses through outreach campaigns but positions as a cold outreach agency. Polza Agency (polzaagency.ru) uses HADI+ICE hypothesis frameworks but for messaging optimization, not product validation. **The "product-led outreach" positioning is essentially absent from the Russian market.**

### How close are custdev agencies to what FirstLeads does?

**Not close.** The gap is structural, not just superficial. Custdev agencies answer "what do people say they want?" through interviews and surveys. FirstLeads would answer "will people actually engage when offered this product?" through real outreach. **No custdev agency identified provides buying signals (willingness to pay through actual engagement).** All operate in the interview → analysis → recommendations paradigm. IT-Agency's "solution interviews" are the closest — they show solutions and ask "are you ready to buy?" — but this is still stated intent collected in an artificial interview setting, not revealed preference from real market interaction. The price range for custdev services (**33,000–550,000+ RUB**) reflects the interview-based cost structure, not outreach execution costs.

## Conclusion: the market gap is real and structural

The research confirms three things with high confidence. First, **"validation through sales" does not exist as a recognized service category** — the specific combination of lean validation methodology with cold outreach execution has no direct competitor in Russia and only two partial analogs globally (Predictable Revenue's sub-program, SalesRoads' secondary use case). Second, the adjacent markets are large but fundamentally different: custdev agencies collect stated opinions, leadgen agencies optimize for pipeline volume, product consultants produce strategy documents, and venture studios take equity for end-to-end company building. None deliver what FirstLeads promises — structured evidence of whether real companies will engage with a product idea, gathered through actual market interaction. Third, the manual workarounds all break down on the same failure mode: they require the founder to either do the outreach themselves (consuming months of time) or hire someone who optimizes for meetings, not insights. The Russian market adds a layer of complexity — global outreach tools don't work well for Russian email providers and B2B databases, creating additional friction for DIY approaches and additional defensibility for a specialized service.

The Russian cold outreach market at **100–120M RUB/year** is small, but 2–3× growth in 2025 indicates a market that's waking up. The ecosystem of Russian tools (Coldy.ai, Respondo.ru, DealRocket, Контур.Компас) is maturing, reducing the infrastructure barrier. The opportunity sits in the framing: the same activities that leadgen agencies perform — cold outreach, ICP testing, messaging iteration — are repackaged from "lead generation" into "product hypothesis validation," targeting a different buyer (product leaders at ideation stage) at a different moment (before committing to build) with a different deliverable (structured evidence of market interest or structured rejection analysis, not just a pipeline of leads).
