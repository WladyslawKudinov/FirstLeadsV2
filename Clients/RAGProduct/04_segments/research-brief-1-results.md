# RAGProduct market research: 8 segments scored for Russian SMB beachhead selection

**The most promising beachhead segments are wholesale/distribution (Segment 1) and service companies with expert bottleneck (Segment 3), based on validated pain points, addressable market size, and competitive white space.** Russia's AI integration market is nascent — only **5.8%** of companies use AI with measurable business effect, yet **34%** are actively experimenting. This gap between interest and execution defines RAGProduct's core opportunity: a structured implementation methodology, not just another tool. Across all 8 segments, pricing at 750K RUB implementation + 80–110K/month positions RAGProduct in the underserved premium-SMB tier — above simple chatbots but below enterprise custom development.

## Segment 1: Wholesale trading and distributors — HIGH PRIORITY

### 1.1 Market size and dynamics

Russia has approximately **534,000 registered wholesale companies** (OKVED 46.x), making wholesale the #1 category by legal entity count. The target sub-segment — revenue 100–500M RUB, 30–200 employees — is estimated at **5,000–15,000 companies**. This range derives from filtering the ~534K total down to established small/medium enterprises, which typically comprise 2–5% of total registered entities. Exact counts require paid databases (SPARK-Interfax, Rusprofile filtered queries).

The sector is **growing**: wholesale SMB turnover rose **+17% in H1 2024** versus H1 2023, with ~30,000 new companies opened in 2024. Wholesale accounts for 15.6% of net profit in the Russian economy. Geographic concentration is high — Moscow/Moscow Oblast, St. Petersburg, and major regional hubs (Novosibirsk, Yekaterinburg, Kazan) dominate.

### 1.2 Problem validation

The pain point is strongly validated by multiple sources:

- Sales managers spend only **28–34% of working time on actual selling** (Salesforce data cited by SalesAI). A Russian field study of 43 sales departments found managers average **2 hours 23 minutes per day** on direct client communication — just 30% of their workday.
- **70% of a manager's time** goes to CRM data entry, planning meetings (~42 min/day), report preparation, commercial proposal generation, order tracking, and correspondence.
- SalesAI estimates AI can free up **2–3 hours/day per manager**, and managers spend up to **40% of working time on routine** instead of client engagement.
- HH.ru job postings for "менеджер по продажам (оптовая торговля)" consistently list duties heavily weighted toward routine: processing requests, generating КП (commercial proposals), CRM maintenance, document preparation, order tracking.

### 1.3 Current solutions and budgets

**CRM systems dominating wholesale**: Bitrix24 (9M+ registered companies; from 2,490 RUB/month), AmoCRM (with AmoAI assistant), 1С:CRM/Управление торговлей (backbone ERP for wholesale), МойСклад (from 495 RUB/month, popular for wholesale+warehouse), BPMSoft (#1 in TAdviser/Skolkovo 2024 CRM rating), SberCRM.

**CRM market**: Russian CRM market exceeded **32 billion RUB in 2024** (+15% YoY). Penetration is only **~14%** of Russian companies. Domestic CRM share: ~90%. Bitrix24 implementation: from **42,000 RUB** (basic) to several hundred thousand RUB (custom). **AI features are being bundled** into existing CRMs (Bitrix24 CoPilot, AmoCRM AmoAI) — a competitive threat.

**IT budgets**: SME IT spending in Russia was **54.8 billion RUB** in 2024. For a wholesale company with 300–500M RUB revenue, estimated IT budget is **3–15M RUB/year** (1–3% of revenue).

### 1.4 Buyer profile

**Primary decision-maker**: Коммерческий директор (Commercial Director) — the #2 person after CEO in most wholesale SMBs. Salary: 150–400K RUB/month (Moscow: up to 650K+). Responsibilities explicitly include "внедрение новых инструментов продаж (digital, CRM, автоматизация)." In smaller companies (30–50 employees), the owner/CEO decides directly.

**Key KPIs**: Revenue growth, sales conversion rate, average deal size, sales cycle length, customer retention, profit margin, deals per manager (team productivity). Budget approval for 500K+ RUB decisions typically requires CEO/owner sign-off. Decision cycle: **1–3 months** from demo to implementation.

## Segment 2: Manufacturing with office block — HIGH PRIORITY

### 2.1 Market size and dynamics

Total manufacturing companies (OKVED 25–30) in Russia: approximately **120,000–150,000** registered entities. The target segment — 50–300 employees, revenue 200–1000M RUB — is estimated at **8,000–12,000 companies**. Russia has ~21,000 medium enterprises total (101–250 employees, up to 2B RUB revenue), with manufacturing (Section C) representing 15–20% of all SMEs.

The digitalization market for manufacturing is approximately **₽60 billion currently**, projected to reach **₽587.5 billion by 2030**. Only **20% of Russian industrial enterprises use AI** versus 60% in leading economies. Russian share of new IT projects in manufacturing: **≥70%**, driven by import substitution mandates (Presidential Decree #166 requires transition to Russian software for KII entities).

Companies with revenue above 200M RUB in machinery/metalworking almost certainly have engineering departments. Presence of CAD users (KOMPAS-3D, SolidWorks, T-FLEX) and PDM implementations confirms engineering offices.

### 2.2 Problem validation

- Engineers spend up to **60% of working time** on non-core tasks: searching for information, coordinating, and "work about work" (Asana research cited by TEAMLY).
- The "knowledge in people's heads" problem is well-documented: information stored in personal folders, chats, and email; no single source of truth; newcomers constantly ask the same questions; regulations become outdated and nobody trusts them.
- Full lifecycle PLM implementation remains **"isolated cases"** in Russian industry — most companies rely on informal knowledge transfer.
- Engineer onboarding: estimated **3–6 months for basic adaptation, 6–12 months for full productivity**.
- HH.ru job postings for "инженер-конструктор" heavily emphasize documentation tasks: developing design documentation per ESKD standards, change notices, 3D modeling, interaction with production — confirming documentation search/creation is central to these roles.

### 2.3 Current solutions and budgets

**Documentation storage**: File servers (still most common at SMBs), 1С:Документооборот/1С:ERP, PDM systems (ЛОЦМАН:PLM by ASKON, T-FLEX DOCs by TopSystems, Союз-PLM), SharePoint (being replaced). Email and messenger sharing is extremely common.

**PDM penetration in medium manufacturing**: estimated **15–25%**. The majority still rely on file servers. ASKON (dominant domestic vendor) reported **₽6.6B revenue in 2024** (+18%), indicating growing adoption.

**ЛОЦМАН:PLM implementation cost** (20–50 users): licenses 1–3M RUB + implementation 2–5M RUB = **total 3–8M RUB** — significantly more expensive than RAGProduct's 750K implementation. For a 500M RUB revenue company, IT budget is estimated at **5–15M RUB/year** (1–3% of revenue).

### 2.4 Buyer profile

**Decision-maker**: At SMB manufacturers (50–300 employees), the **owner or General Director** makes final IT purchasing decisions, with strong input from the **Technical Director/Chief Engineer**. Dedicated CIO/IT Director is uncommon at this size. CTO salary: from **600K RUB/month**.

**CTO KPIs**: Production volume/timeline/quality, target cost of goods, operational profit, time-to-market for new products, defect rate. **Strong preference for on-premise** deployment — especially for engineering data and any defense-related work.

### 2.5 Access channels

- **Associations**: Союз машиностроителей России, РСПП, Ассоциация Цифровых Технологий в Промышленности (АЦТП), Деловая Россия

## Segment 3: Service companies with expert bottleneck — HIGH PRIORITY

### 3.1 Market size and dynamics

**Accounting outsourcing**: 30,000+ companies providing accounting services; 9,472 new firms registered in Jan–Nov 2025 (OKVED 69.2, maximum since 2019, +11% YoY). RAEX reports top-83 accounting outsourcers earned ₽18.7B (+14% YoY). **Law firms**: ~800 active firms with revenue >50M RUB; cumulative revenue ₽175.9B. **HR outsourcing**: 3,237 companies (+7.2% YoY); total staffing/BPO market ₽561.7B in 2024 (+18%).

**Target segment estimate (20–100 employees)**: **2,500–5,000 firms** total across accounting (2,000–4,000), legal (300–500), and HR outsourcing (200–400).

**Growth drivers**: Regulatory burden increases, FNS scrutiny, labor shortage (кадровый дефицит), VAT rate changes, lowered simplified taxation thresholds. Growth: **+10–15% annually** for accounting outsourcing, **+18–20%** for HR/staffing outsourcing.

### 3.2 Problem validation

The expert bottleneck is **strongly validated** from multiple angles:

- Rabrain.ru (knowledge base lab) explicitly targets this problem: claims **+30% processing speed, −40% onboarding costs, adaptation from 3 months → 4 weeks, 50+ hours/month saved** on knowledge transfer. Their pitch: "horizontal scaling — quickly grow staff without quality loss."
- The "Василий Васильевич" problem (Doczilla case study): lawyers waste hours asking senior colleagues rather than using structured systems — but "Василий Васильевич" can only consult one person at a time.
- **86% of lawyers experience burnout** (Baker McKenzie + Pravo.ru). AI can automate **up to 23% of standard corporate lawyer tasks** (McKinsey). Document preparation time reduced **6x** with AI tools (from 15 min to 2.5 min per document).
- Only **14.5% of Russian legal professionals actively use GenAI** tools; 33.9% plan to start. But 88% of lawyers use neural networks in some capacity (Avito/Pravo.ru, Dec 2025) — indicating openness to AI.

### 3.3 Current solutions and budgets

- **ConsultantPlus**: Dominant legal reference (330M+ documents). Pricing not publicly disclosed — estimated **₽20,000–120,000/year** depending on package and user count
- **Гарант**: Competitor to ConsultantPlus; has integrated "Нейроюрист" AI feature
- **1С:Бухгалтерия / 1С:Аналитика**: Backbone for accounting; AI-powered anomaly detection in 1С:Аналитика
- **СБИС**: AI financial analysis, ЭДО, counterparty checks
- **Doczilla Pro/AI**: Document constructor with AI editing, CLM. From **₽1,700/month per user** (personal) to ₽3,500–10,000/month (business tiers). For 10 users ≈ ₽35K–100K/month
- **Noroots**: AI contract review. Reduces review time by ~30%. Pricing: individual
- **Банк Точка**: Free AI assistant for accountant clients

**IT budget estimate** for 20–100 person firm: **₽300K–800K/year** (ConsultantPlus + 1C + basic IT). Annual RAGProduct cost (₽750K impl + ~₽1.2M/yr subscription) = **₽1.8–2.1M first year**, representing ~1.5–3% of typical firm revenue (50–200M RUB range).

### 3.4 Buyer profile

**Decision-maker**: Managing Partner / Senior Partner (law firms) or General Director / Owner (accounting outsourcing). No dedicated IT role in most 20–100 person firms. Price sensitivity is **HIGH** — AI must demonstrate clear ROI. **Key trigger events**: scaling pains, quality issues, regulatory changes, staff turnover.

## Segment 5: IT companies — MEDIUM PRIORITY

### 5.1 Market size

Total IT entities: **~256,000** (mid-2025); **20,000+ accredited**. IT market: **₽4.4 trillion** (2024), growing **17%** in H1. Companies specifically with 20–150 employees: estimated **5,000–15,000**. IT revenues exceeded ₽13 trillion. IT contributes **6% of GDP**.

### 5.2 Problem validation

Documentation problems are real — the Confluence/Notion exit from Russia (fully blocked September 2024) created acute knowledge management debt. Developers spend only **19 hours/week on actual coding**; rest on meetings, admin, and documentation search. Onboarding lasts **3–12 months**. Companies scrambled for alternatives; one Habr-documented case used "crutches and exports" for 3 years waiting for a Confluence replacement.

### 5.3 Current solutions

Post-sanctions landscape is fragmented:

- **Blocked/unavailable**: Notion, Confluence, Miro (paid), ChatGPT (officially)
- **Russian alternatives**: Yandex 360 for Business (**559–1,399 RUB/mo per user**), TEAMLY (Confluence replacement with AI), Kaiten (free knowledge base), Yonote (Notion analog), EvaWiki
- **AI APIs**: GigaChat (**0.065–0.65 RUB/1K tokens**), YandexGPT (0.2–2 RUB/1K tokens)

### 5.4–5.6 Critical assessment

**Build-vs-buy is THE barrier**: IT companies are biased toward "build," can use GigaChat/YandexGPT APIs directly, and will benchmark against their own capabilities. At 80–110K/month for a 50-person company (**~2,000–5,500 RUB/employee/month**), this competes with cheaper alternatives (Yandex 360 at 559–1,399/user, TEAMLY at 199/user). Internal RAG build estimated at 500K–1.5M RUB development + 50–100K/month maintenance.

**Verdict**: Market exists but is **niche and challenging**. Best sub-segment: outsourcing/service companies with 50–150 employees and high turnover, lacking in-house AI expertise. Pure product companies with senior engineering teams are unlikely buyers.

## Segment 6: Government organizations — MEDIUM PRIORITY

### 6.1 Market size

GUPs/MUPs: approximately **6,000–7,000** remaining as of 2025 (down from 18,624 in 2018), with reform mandating liquidation of those on competitive markets. Addressable organizations including surviving GUPs/MUPs plus subordinate бюджетные/автономные учреждения: estimated **2,000–5,000** with 50–500 employees.

Government IT procurement (2023): **₽429 billion** (276.7B under 44-FZ + 152.6B under 223-FZ). AI procurement in 2025: **₽1.8 billion** (7.4x growth YoY). The new national project "Экономика данных" (2025–2030) allocates **₽1.43 trillion total**, with **₽475 billion** for "Цифровое государственное управление."

### 6.2 Problem validation

Document approval previously took ~4 days on average; now reduced to ~1.5 days through digital management. Government organizations process massive volumes of citizen appeals under FZ-59. Key pains: large volume of routine correspondence, responsibility dilution across departments, delayed processing, expensive integration with existing systems. **80% face IT/cybersecurity personnel shortages**.

### 6.3 Current solutions and critical requirements

- **Directum RX**: From **₽450,000 for 50 users**; FSTEC/FSB certified
- **1С:Документооборот**: From **₽42,500–55,300** (box); additional users from 7,000 RUB/seat
- **ELMA365**: From **500 RUB/month** or 12,000 RUB permanent per seat

**CRITICAL BARRIERS**:
- **Must be listed in Реестр отечественного ПО** (27,000+ products; mandatory for government)
- **FSTEC certification** required for restricted-access data
- **Data residency**: Only Russian servers (ФЗ-242)
- **New FSTEC Order №117** (effective March 1, 2026): dramatically expands scope to ALL government organizations including ГУПs and учреждения
- **Procurement via 44-ФЗ/223-ФЗ**: Formal tenders, 1–3 month procurement cycle, 6–18 month implementation timeline

### 6.4–6.6 Buyer, channels, competitors

**Decision-maker**: Organization director (final budget authority) + IT director/deputy. **Budget planning**: Annual cycle, requests prepared April–September for following year. **Key events**: ЦИПР (May 19–22, 2026, Nizhny Novgorod — 15,000+ attendees, 300+ agreements), CNews Forums. **Competitors**: Rostelecom (₽122B in government digital services), Sber AI (GigaChat/SaluteBot), Directum (built-in AI), ELMA365 AI, CorpGPT (in Реестр ПО #23046 — cleared for 44-FZ/223-FZ procurement).

**Assessment**: Massive budgets but **extremely high barriers to entry**. Long sales cycles, certification requirements, and procurement formalities make this segment challenging for a startup without existing government credentials.

## Cross-segment findings that reshape the opportunity

### AI integration is an emerging, not established, market category

Russian companies spent **₽90.3 billion on AI implementation in 2024** (FinExpertiza). The AI market ranges from ₽130–305 billion depending on methodology. "AI integration methodology" is **not yet a formalized category** but is forming organically — companies like Onellect, Resolventa Group (from 3,500 RUB/hour), UMVEK (from 500K RUB), SoftwareCenter (from 100K RUB), and Salekit position as AI integrators offering structured approaches. RAGProduct could help **define and own** this emerging category.

### The gap between AI interest and real adoption is enormous

**43% of organizations** claim to use AI (Sber, 2024), but only **5.8% achieve measurable business effect** (FinExpertiza, Feb 2026). Meanwhile, **34% are actively implementing** and 18% plan to start in 2025. Russia ranks **119th of 147 countries** in Microsoft's AI Adoption Index. The 28-percentage-point gap between experimentation and real results is RAGProduct's core market — companies need structured methodology, not just tool access.

### Willingness to pay clusters around ₽75K/month for SMBs

Average SMB AI implementation budget: **₽75,000/month** (vc.ru, 50 Russian cases). Over **60% of companies** spend less than 1% of their digital budget on AI. **96–100% of business AI financing** comes from companies' own funds.
